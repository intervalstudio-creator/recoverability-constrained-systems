Boundary - Public Evaluation Layer

DOI: https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.19674392

Boundary evaluates recoverability and produces admissibility states.

It does not make, enforce, or autonomously execute clinical, legal, financial, operational, or institutional decisions.

All outputs require action by a responsible human authority.

## Core Principle

A system may evaluate, simulate, or recommend without restriction.

A system may only act where recoverability can be established in time under real conditions as sufficient to prevent irreversible transition.

If recoverability cannot be established:

- continuation is non-admissible
- execution does not occur

## What Boundary Does

Boundary is a public evaluation and visibility layer.

It helps identify when continuation may no longer be recoverable in time under real conditions and provides a structured way to represent that state.

Boundary is intended to support:

- visibility
- review
- escalation awareness
- public understanding of recoverability conditions

It is not a real-world control, enforcement, or autonomous execution system.

## Output States

Boundary produces exactly four states:

- CONTINUE - recoverability appears established
- DEGRADED - one or more conditions appear weakened
- NON-ADMISSIBLE - recoverability cannot be established
- NON-EXECUTABLE - continuation is non-admissible and urgent human review is required

These outputs are informational and evaluative. They do not replace human judgment, professional authority, or formal decision-making.

## Positioning

Boundary is designed as a recoverability evaluation framework for public, educational, analytical, and review purposes.

It does not:

- provide medical advice
- replace clinicians, lawyers, regulators, or other responsible authorities
- autonomously control real-world systems
- execute or trigger external actions
- provide operational enforcement capability
- function as a deployment blueprint for institutional control systems

## Public Scope

This public release is intended to provide:

- a conceptual overview
- a public-facing evaluation interface
- plain-language understanding of recoverability states
- simplified examples across domains

Certain implementation details are intentionally not included in the public release.

This includes operational, enforcement, orchestration, integration, timing, and deployment mechanisms whose unrestricted publication could enable misuse in contexts where recoverability cannot be ensured.

## High-Level Evaluation Basis

Boundary evaluates whether recoverability appears to remain present under real conditions.

At a public level, this includes questions such as whether:

- a recovery path appears to exist
- that path appears reachable
- failure appears detectable in time
- response appears possible in time
- recovery appears executable in time
- irreversible transition appears preventable before recovery

If these cannot be established, continuation is treated as non-admissible.

Unknown or unestablished critical conditions are not treated as safe.

## Interface

The public interface is designed to support:

- simple evaluation flows
- plain-language interpretation
- educational understanding of recoverability states
- public demonstration of admissibility logic

Where public tools are provided, they are evaluative only.

They do not execute actions, connect to live institutional systems, or provide operational enforcement capability.

## Public Safety Boundary

This repository and its public materials are valid only insofar as they preserve the recoverability constraint.

Any implementation that:

- removes recoverability verification
- weakens bounded review or human accountability
- simulates admissibility without real recoverability
- converts the framework into an autonomous enforcement or exclusion system

is non-admissible and invalid.
